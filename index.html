"Understanding Big Data"

By Zain Mohammed

Big data means huge amounts of information that come from many places like phones, websites, apps and sensors. It’s so big that one computer can’t handle it, so the data is stored and worked on by many computers at once. This helps find patterns, like what people buy or how traffic moves. The data is cleaned and checked so its useful. Big data is used to help make smarter choices and improve things we use every day. It’s also important to keep people’s information safe and private.

Here’s how big data usually works:

  • Collect: from apps, sensors and websites.

  • Store: in big servers or cloud systems.

  • Process: using many computers.

  • Analyse: to find patterns and trends.

  • Use: for better services and decision making.
